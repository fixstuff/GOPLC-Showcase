Part VII gathers the public technical whitepapers that expand on the deeper design decisions behind GoPLC. Unlike the reference chapters in earlier parts, whitepapers argue a position: that Go is a credible language for real-time control, that a single PLC runtime can credibly span from a washing machine to a datacenter power room, that industrial automation patterns translate to humanoid robotics, and that commodity hardware can meet the requirements modern operators are throwing at it. Read these last — or first, if you prefer your reference material framed by the arguments that motivated it.
